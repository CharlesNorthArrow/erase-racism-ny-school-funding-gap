Long Island: Extreme Inequity in Plain Sight

Together the two counties enroll 418,302 students and face a combined funding gap of nearly $6.9 billion — $2.7 billion in Nassau and $4.2 billion in Suffolk. Both counties currently receive far less than the updated formula would entitle them to, at 186% and 193% of current Foundation Aid respectively. But at the district level, we see that the island is home to extreme inequities, with some districts facing a per-pupil funding gap of upwards of $40,000 while others are currently receiving more money than the updated formula would allow.

About This Analysis

This interactive visualization presents findings from ERASE Racism's March 2026 report on New York State's Foundation Aid formula. The analysis quantifies the funding gap between what districts receive under the current formula and what they would receive under four proposed updates.
